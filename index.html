About Me

Who Am I?

I’ve always loved a good challenge. Whether it’s solving a tough problem or stepping outside my comfort zone, I find energy in pushing my boundaries and figuring things out. I enjoy thinking creatively, learning continuously, and working on problems until I find a solution that works.

As a team leader, I’m adaptable and collaborative-focused not just on the end goal, but also on helping fellow team-mates grow through the process. Through experiences like playing in the SST Guitar Ensemble and working on group projects, I’ve learnt to value feedback, stay open to new ideas, and appreciate different perspectives. These trials and tribulations have shaped how I work, lead, and learn.

ElderGuide

For my year-end Capstone project, I collaborated with Heartware Network alongside 3 friends to help Singapore’s pioneer generation embrace technology. Through this meaningful experience, I have developed greater patience, compassion, and understanding. I also gained valuable insight into the lives of elderly individuals across Singapore and the often unseen challenges they face daily.

2023 NUS Chem•Comm competition

In 2023, I had the opportunity to participate in the NUS Chem•Comm Competition, where my team focused on researching the impact of Carbon Capture Units (CCUs) as a strategy to help schools achieve carbon neutrality by 2030. You can visit the website we designed for the competition here.

ISS

For this projection, our goal was to investigate the effect of colour on the absorption of infrared radiation. This project marked my first full scientific investigation, where I was actively involved in every stage, from designing the experimental setup and conducting the tests, to gathering data and analyzing the results.

ARTC camp

During the ARTC camp, we were challenged to design a product that could enhance the effectiveness of the recycling process. My team worked together to develop a prototype for a sensor-based device that could detect whether bottles were properly cleaned before being allowed to be placed in the recycling bin. This would help prevent contamination, improving the overall efficiency and quality of recycling efforts.

2023 SYF competition

I was proud to be part of the SST Guitar Ensemble, which was awarded Distinction at the 2023 Singapore Youth Festival (SYF) Competition. This experience was a golden opportunity for me to challenge myself by learning and performing complex musical pieces that demanded skill, focus, and discipline.

2024 Performance at Istana

The SST Guitar Ensemble was invited to perform at the Istana for the Hari Raya Haji. It was a very eye-opening experience for me as it was a chance for us to perform in front of a live audience. It was not an easy feat. We had to learn quite a number of pieces as our slot was for one hour, and we also had to perform outdoors in the sweltering heat.

2024 SUTD-SST-NP Big D Camp

The SUTD-SST-NP Big D Camp taught me how to work with my team members to come up with a prototype in a short period of time. Over 3 days, we had to keep focused on the task at hand as we had limited time to agree on an idea, come up with the product design and build the prototype.

IDC challenge

This challenge gave me the opportunity to work with my team-mates to build a disaster rescue robot. We worked on integrating an AI run on Raspberry Pi with the movement and control of the robot and sensors by making use of an Arduino Uno.

YCEP

Over the past three years, I have taken part in multiple YCEP events organised by CSA and different polytechnics. This allowed me to pick up useful cybersecurity skills that help me to learn to be safe when being online.

Computing performance task

My assigned Computing Performance Task was to use the Python programming and facial recognition libraries to detect the faces of users. The context was for it be a virtual photobooth for SST’s anniversary, hence eliminating the need for physical props. Link to the github repo.
